Microtomographic study of Olenelloid trilobite guts

Supervised by Javier Ortega-Hernández and Rudy Lerosey-Aubril · Havard University · 2024

This work was presented at GSA 2024 (view abstract)

Overview

This project investigated internal anatomical structures in exceptionally preserved trilobites from the Lower Cambrian of Canada (Rosella Formation). These rare specimens preserve soft tissues, including parts of the digestive system, offering a unique window into early arthropod anatomy. The aim was to use non-destructive CT imaging to reconstruct and study gut morphology in three dimensions.

One of the reconstructed gut models (shown in pink) →

Methods

Specimens were first screened using radiography, then scanned with high-resolution CT. Volumetric datasets were processed in ImageJ, and low-contrast gut structures were segmented using Dragonfly and ParaView. 3D reconstructions were refined in Blender to produce detailed models, figures, and animations.

Trilobite fossil → CT scan → 3D reconstruction of the gut

Results

Gut structures were reconstructed in 25 specimens, allowing a detailed anatomical description. The digestive system shows a differentiated anterior crop and a simpler posterior tract. In one specimen, preserved gut contents, interpreted as a phosphatocopina arthropod, provide rare direct evidence of trophic interactions. These findings offer new insights into feeding strategies and the early evolution of trophic networks in arthropods.
